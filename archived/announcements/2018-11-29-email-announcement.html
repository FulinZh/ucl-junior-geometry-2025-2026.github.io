Title: A Horizontal Introduction to Stacks

Thu 29.11.18

17.00-18.00

Location: UCL Maths D103

Abstract: Algebraic stacks are an enlargement of the category of schemes. They most naturally appear whenever the problem of "too many automorphisms" prevents the existence of moduli spaces, e.g. for parameter spaces of curves. Instead of engaging in a hand-waving story, my talk will focus on the technical machinery behind stacks so that when the time comes you can say "quasi-separated Deligne-Mumford stack" with confidence.

Keywords: fine moduli space, Grothendieck topology, 2-category, descent theory
